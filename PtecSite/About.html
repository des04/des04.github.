About PTEC:

PTEC

PTEC stands for Pembina Trails Early College and is an advanced technologically focused education program for highschool students within the Pembina Trails School Division. Students within the program are taught software, hardware, cyber security and cloud administration skills while being imersed in a world of innovation and technology at the University Of Manitoba. Students who attend PTEC take core courses at their regular highschools before or after being bussed to PTEC.

Grade 9

Grade nine students are taught how to code C# using unity and visual studio. By the end of the first half of the school year, students are capable of creating 2D video games. In the second half of the year, students are taught networking essentials, allowing them to attain a solid understanding of internetworks.

 

Grade 10

Grade ten students expand their software development knowlege, but in a web focused fashion as opposed to a video game focused fashion. Grade tens learn HTML, CSS, JavaScript as well as web design priciples in the first half of the school year. By this point, they are capable of creating fully asthetic, interactive websites. While grade 10 students are learning web development at school (on top of their core courses), they also must take an online course in IT essentials. This course teaches hardware on top of many other things to students. In the second half of the year, grade tens expand their knowledge on networking, improving in terms of cyber security and cloud administration. It is also around this time that students are paired with companies for internships. At the end of grade ten, students will choose which field to specialize in for their grade eleven and twelve years. The two fields being Software Development and Cyber Security and Cloud Administration.

Grades 11 and 12

It is at this point that students have selected a stream that they will specialize in at the Manitoba Institute of Trades and Technology. The two avaible streams are Software Development or Cyber Security and Cloud Administration. The courses that the students will take at The Manitoba Institude of Trades and Technology (communly reffered to as M.I.T.T.) would normally cost an individual eight thousand dollars, however, for students who went to PTEC the courses are completely free. Upon graduation, students will have elite-level diplomas as well as advanced workplace skills.
